How we choose our picks

We don’t run timed trials or build our own test rigs. Instead, we pore over long‑term owner reviews on forums, paddling clubs, and retail sites to see which carts hold up after a season of real use, and which ones break. We also cross‑reference that feedback with published assessments from seasoned kayak guides and outdoor gear reviewers who actually drag carts through sand, mud, and grass week after week. The main things that separate a good cart from a bad one are the wheels (airless vs. pneumatic, diameter, tread), the frame’s weight capacity and corrosion resistance, and how easily it stows. We look for patterns: if a cart’s axle bends under heavy loads or its saddles scratch hulls, that’s a red flag we flag here. Our goal is to steer you toward options that have earned a reputation for reliability, not just flashy marketing.
