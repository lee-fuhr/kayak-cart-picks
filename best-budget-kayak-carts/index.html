A kayak cart turns a back-straining portage into a two-minute stroll. For paddlers on a budget, the trick is finding a cart that won’t buckle under a heavy hull or wobble on loose sand, without spending as much as you did on your boat. The three models here cover the sweet spot: each costs less than a tank of gas for a week of trips, yet they differ dramatically in wheel size, folding mechanism, and carrying capacity. Storage space matters too, some carts collapse small enough to stash in a hatch, while others stay assembled and ride in your truck bed. We’ve ranked these by real-world owner satisfaction and expert consensus, not by lab tests we didn’t run.

Our verdict

No single cart suits every paddler, but the trio above covers the range.
