Hauling a kayak from car to launch is often the most awkward part of a paddle trip. A good cart turns that struggle into a smooth, one-person job, saving your back and protecting your hull. But not every cart works on every surface or fits every boat, cheap plastic wheels can sink in soft sand, and oversize models might not stow in your trunk. To find the best kayak carts for 2026, we dug into owner reviews, expert tests from paddling publications, and forum discussions across a range of hull types and launching conditions. Every pick here balances portability, durability, and wheel performance for its price band. Pay attention to tire type (airless vs. pneumatic), weight capacity, and how the cart attaches, straps, cradles, or simple pins, because that determines how quickly you can set up and how secure your kayak feels on the roll.
